First ¿What is a Tree?

A plant having a permanently woody main stem or trunk, ordinarily growing to a considerable height, and usually developing branches at some distance from the ground.

In botany, a tree is a perennial plant with an elongated stem, or trunk, usually supporting branches and leaves.



Trees

Cactus

With close to 150 varieties of cactus (or nopal), various cacti can be found right across the country, from the urban hub of the capital to the vacant deserts of the northern territories.

Maguey

The maguey plant from which we get mezcal, tequila and pulque as well as useful fibres like henequen and sisal.

Pitaya

The pitaya plant is technically part of the aforementioned cactus family, but due to the entirely unique and delicious fruit it bears we thought it was worthy of its own entry.

Cempasuchil

Cempasuchiles are the richly pigmented orange blooms associated with Mexico’s famed Day of the Dead celebrations, making October and November the perfect months of the year to see them decorating public and private spaces across Mexico.

Jacarandas

Spring is often considered the best time to visit the Mexican capital, and that is surely due in part to the wealth of jacarandas that bloom across the city.

Importance of Tree

Forests are of immense importance in soil stabilization and erosion control, especially in mountainous and hilly regions; they also protect and conserve water supplies and prevent floods.
